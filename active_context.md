## Established Facts

### Portfolio & Lifecycle Structure
- Total revenue: EUR 62.5M (Jan 2021–Apr 2023); FY2021 EUR 16.7M → FY2022 EUR 32.8M (+96.5%) → LTM23 EUR 36.1M (+66.1% vs prior LTM); CAGR (N=2.25y) ≈ +39%.
- Growth is overwhelmingly volume-driven: FY21→FY22 volume effect +92.6%, price/mix +7.4%; LTM22→LTM23 volume +102.5%, price/mix -2.5% (ASP dilution at scale).
- New Entrant models (absent prior periods) represent 50–55% of LTM23 revenue (EUR 18–20M, 109 SKUs) — the dominant lifecycle bucket and primary growth engine; Stable models only 27.4% (EUR 9.9M, 39 SKUs); Bust declining from EUR 8.45M (LTM22) to EUR 2.06M (LTM23) = EUR 6.4M headwind absorbed by new launches.
- 97% of New Entrant models are already past peak (median time-to-peak = month 3). Post-peak retention: 45% at +1m, 32% at +2m, 26% at +3m, 17% at +6m; only 4.4% retain >50% of peak revenue at +6m.
- Durability segmentation (97 NE models with data): 18.6% Durable (≥40% retention at +3m, EUR 4.60M LTM), 33.0% Mid-tier, 48.5% Transient (<20% retention, EUR 6.27M LTM). Neither launch ASP (r=–0.087) nor launch velocity (r=+0.119) meaningfully predicts durability. Lowest ASP quartile (avg EUR 23) yields highest Durable rate at 36%; highest ASP quartile (avg EUR 60) only 16.7%.
- Top Durable NE models: SEOUL (EUR 926K, 85% retention at +3m), MILWAUKEE (EUR 729K), ATLANTIC CITY (EUR 445K). Top Transient: EDINBURGH (EUR 516K, 18% retention), GREENLAND (EUR 247K, 4%), GRAND PLACE (EUR 227K, 10%).
- Stable models: median active lifespan 18m vs 7m for NE; peak extends to month 25, indicating compounding revenue. Bust cohort mirrors NE decay patterns — many current NEs will migrate to Bust.
- Top 20 models = 39% of LTM23 revenue; no single model >3.8% share. Top 15 = 28.9% of cumulative revenue. Portfolio breadth = 398 models.

### Product Segmentation (ASP & Entry Tier)
- ASP tiers: Entry (<EUR 48, EUR 14.3M LTM23, 71.5% WS-dependent); Lower-Mid (EUR 48–55); Upper-Mid (EUR 55–65); Premium (>EUR 65, EUR ~5M, 84.2% Stable rate, 43.9% Online / 35.9% Retail / 20.2% WS).
- Entry-tier NE models generate EUR 8.84M LTM23 vs Stable Entry EUR 4.90M — an anomalous inversion absent in Premium tier (NE EUR 383K vs Stable EUR 3.2M). Entry NE is 73.4% WS-dependent (FY2022: 80.8%), confirming sell-in dynamics.
- Entry NE average +3m retention = 27.4% vs 34.9% for Stable; 15 of 38 Entry NE models are Transient (30.4% of NE Entry revenue = EUR 2.69M at risk). Only 6 qualify as Durable.
- FY2022 H1 Entry NE cohort: peaked EUR 2.1M in Q3 2022, decayed to EUR 188K by Q2 2023 (–91%). FY2022 H2 cohort replicated spike-and-decay. Confirms rolling cohort substitution model.
- Repeat client rate: NE Entry 26.2% vs Stable Entry 36.9%; ordering frequency 1.45m vs 1.95m.
- Only 3 NE Entry models launched in LTM23 (vs 32 in FY2022) — new-model pipeline is slowing.
- Model naming is city-based (no viable family taxonomy): 70.1% single-word models; only 31 prefixes span 2+ models (EUR 3.84M, 10.6% of LTM23 revenue). Compound/collection models declined –3.5% in LTM23 vs +12.4% for single-word models.
- Lower ASP tiers drove FY2021–FY2022 surge: Entry +204%, Lower-Mid +136% vs Premium +39% — volume-led, not value-led.

### Wholesale Channel & Client Dynamics
- Wholesale = 59–65% of total revenue across all periods; LTM23 WS revenue EUR 21.43M.
- WS NE revenue = EUR 12.65M (59% of total WS LTM), up from 4.1% (EUR 0.57M) in PLTM22; Bust collapsed from 54.7% (EUR 7.59M) to 0.9% (EUR 0.19M) — near-complete portfolio rotation.
- WS LTM23 headline growth +6.5% (EUR +1.31M): entirely from new client acquisition (525 new clients, EUR 2.00M); returning-client LfL = –1.6% (EUR 19.74M → EUR 19.42M). Sharp reversal from FY2021→FY2022 LfL of +62.1%.
- FY2021→FY2022 WS growth +84.6% (EUR +9.22M): 777 returning clients grew +62.1% (high-quality organic); lost-client churn only EUR 0.64M.
- WS client base: FY2021 996 → LTM23 1,690 clients (CAGR +26.5%); new client acquisition consistent (507 FY2022, 525 LTM23) but avg revenue per new client declining (EUR 6,883 → EUR 3,810) — smaller/lower-tier accounts being onboarded.
- 2021 cohort retained 78% of clients in FY2022, declining to 53% in LTM23 — accelerating attrition.
- WS concentration (HHI): 0.0140 (FY2021) → 0.0106 (LTM23); top decile (169 clients) = 69% of LTM23 WS revenue (EUR 14.78M). No single-client catastrophic concentration.
- Top 20 WS clients bifurcated: growers include ASH SHOE CO. (+24.2%), BUY SHOES SL (+43.6%), MODIVO (+25.6%); decliners include O MODA SCHOENEN BV (–31.2%), LOOKIERO STYLE SL (–28.0%), FALABELLA RETAIL (–25.4%).
- Declining top-20 clients avg Stable% = 29.9%, NE% = 69.9%; growing clients avg Stable% = 45.7%, NE% = 52.2% — structural basket composition divergence.

### Client Lifecycle Mix & NE Dependency
- Revenue-weighted YoY by Stable% quartile (monotonic): Q1 (0–18% Stable) = –21.2%; Q2 = +31.7%; Q3 = +39.4%; Q4 (39–100%) = +63.0%.
- Inverse gradient for NE% quartiles: Q1 (0–60% NE) = +67.8%; Q2 = +37.8%; Q3 = +19.9%; Q4 (81–124% NE) = –20.7%. Over-concentration in NE models associated with underperformance.
- NE-led clients (n=638, EUR 12.55M LTM): median YoY +3.9% but mean +76.3% — high dispersion driven by outliers (e.g., SIMMS SIGAL LINWOOD +11,316%).
- Bust-heavy clients (n=4): avg YoY –19.6%, 0% growing. Stable-led clients (n=89): avg YoY +75.1%, 64% growing.
- 741 returning WS clients: 718 (97%) in High-NE / Low-Breakout group (G4), EUR 16.64M LTM, +38.1% weighted YoY, 56.3% growing — trickle NE portfolio is the actual WS growth engine.
- 883 new WS clients onboarded in LTM vs 741 returning active from PLTM — significant client base expansion; revenue split not yet quantified.

### NE Model Concentration (Breakout vs Trickle)
- WS NE revenue (EUR 12.65M) is highly fragmented: top model (SEOUL) = 5.4% of NE revenue; 21 models needed for 50% threshold; 51 models for 80% — not concentrated in a small number of breakouts.
- Only 2 breakout NE models (≥EUR 500K): SEOUL (EUR 684K WS) and MILWAUKEE (EUR 522K WS), together = 9.5% of WS NE revenue. Both are entirely new (zero PLTM revenue).
- 113 trickle NE models = 90.5% of WS NE revenue. Breakout model post-peak retention: 67.5% at +3m, collapsing to 9.5% at +6m and 0% at +9m — sharp demand cliff.
- Trickle NE models: 19.6% retention at +3m, 6.7% at +6m — weaker short-term but risk distributed across many SKUs.

### Channel Mix & Other
- Online: single unique client all periods (likely marketplace/aggregator); AOV EUR ~3,958 (LTM23); revenue share declined –6.4pp to 21.7%. Online AOV ≈ 4.8x WS, 10.6x Retail.
- Retail: fastest-growing channel (4 → 63 clients, CAGR +240.5%); revenue share +12.3pp to 19.0%; AOV compressed EUR 1,091 → EUR 373 (client base broadening effect).
- WS AOV improved EUR 633 → EUR 816; consistent with larger basket sizes among existing accounts.
